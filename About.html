Physical fitness

There are numerous benefits of physical fitness which include better health, greater strength, increased flexibility, increased energy, improved appetite, positive attitude, and a good mood.

Mental fitness

Mental fitness is just as important as physical fitness, and shouldn’t be neglected. Including mental dexterity exercises into your daily routine can help you reap the benefits of a sharper mind and a healthier body for years to come.

What is Physical health?

Physical health has two central components:

  • Nutrition: Good nutrition is essential for everyone, but growing bodies need even more resources. A host of vitamins and minerals is essential to physical health, as are the right amounts of protein and carbohydrates.
  • Activity: It’s increasingly challenging to ensure kids get enough exercise and physical activity. With the proliferation of screens in every area of life, children and young adults are becoming more sedentary.

What can do to Improve Physical health?

Here are three terms changes you can make to improve health:

  • Limit screen time
  • Eat healthy food
  • Scheldule time for activities
  • Encourage exercise

What is Mental Health?

The state of somone's mental health has significant sway over the way they act, process emotions and make decisions.

These are some of the signs that someone’s mental health is shifting:

  • Changes in sleeping and eating patterns
  • Withdrawal from friends, family and activities
  • loss of energy
  • Increasing irritability and mood swings
  • Loss of performance at school or work

What can do to Improve mental health?

Start with self-care:

  • Solidify a sleep schedule
  • Get outside
  • Scheldule time for activities
  • Do Meditation
  • Eat healthy
